Something About Me

My name is "Syed Meesam Ali" and I am an experienced Engineer by profession. Computer programming had been my passion since childhood starting from the day I read a book on GW-BASIC. It was an aha moment for me. It was an amazing feeling that the "magic box" (as my uncle used to call it) could be controlled by anyone with knowledge of something called programming. I had a small taste of "C Language" while reading a friend's course book on C-language. Interestingly, though it was part of his course-work but he had no interest in programming while I was enjoying it. Unsurprisingly at one point I was the one teaching him "C-Language (Turbo-C compiler)".

Things changed when I joined university and opted for a course in "Civil Engineering" (due to friends recommendation). I was the most excited one while taking course on Visual Basic in my university's first year. We built a simple calculator and I was helping many of my classmates who were totally new to programming and didn't enjoy it much. For my final year project I choose the project with title (computer program for calculation of forces on "composite bridge girders"). The original program was written using Fortran-77 and I had to convert it to Visual Basic.

I got also deeply interested in electronics and embedded systems when I found out about "8051 Microcontrollers". It got me hooked completely to programming (Assembly & C) as well as electronics. I learnt a lot about MCUs and how to program and interface them to external world. I did a "Simple Temperature Sensor" project using LM35DZ sensor (Display was using 7-segment LEDs). Later I programmed stepper motor by connecting it to LPT printer port of PC and controlling through MATLAB.

After starting my first job as a professional civil engineer there wasn't much use of programming directly. My work mostly involved preparing BOQs for various projects using MS Excel. Always working on excel made me realise that a lot of mundane tasks could be made automated through macros (made in Visual Basic for Applications or VBA).

VBA was fun language to learn and the best part was that I could immediately put it to use. Many simple tasks can be automated with VBA easily. I embarked upon creating my own estimation module i.e. a simple VBA module which will automatically gather data from various sheets and give the aggregate result including calculations on some final results sheet. It was great success for me as it was my first full scale Programming Project.

Best thing that I learnt from my first project was that anything can be AUTOMATED through combination of various programming techniques. I learned VBA for Outlook and this helped me prepare a simple module for automatically saving emails along with their attachments to their own independent folders. In the meanwhile I also started my own "Excel Advanced" page on Facebook where I try to post some advanced excel tips regularly.

Here is my "Excel Advanced" facebook page

Starting in 2018 I started to think seriously about programming due to below reasons.

  • Blockchain became mainstream due to introduction of Bitcoin-mania towards end of 2017
  • I took some edx courses on Python and found it to be most versatile and useful of all
  • Embedded is always something I love. MCUs and hardware is my passion. IoT is nothing but connected embedded devices
  • I started teaching (only to enhance and fine-tune by existing programming knowledge and learn what i didn't know)
  • I took a course on Javascript, C++ and Blockchain Programming from online academy
  • I became serious about transitioning myself full-time IT/Programming career path
